# RoadReady Grade Crossing Controllers

The RoadReady line of model railroad grade crossing controllers from Iowa Scaled Engineering offers a way to bring simple, realistic crossing signal operation to your model railroad.  We offer two versions - the [RoadReady Basic](./Basic Crossing/manual.md) and the [RoadReady Pro](./Pro Crossing/manual.md) - to meet both simple and complex needs.

The **[RoadReady Basic](./Basic Crossing/manual.md)** is designed to provide simple crossing animation and sound.  It provides robust outputs for LED crossing signals and includes a speaker and seven different selectable prototype bell recordings.  It uses two plug-and-play TrainSpotter detectors to trigger the crossing (as a [crossing island circuit](../Tips%20and%20Tricks/Articles/xing-basics.md#island-only-crossing-circuits), with a programmable timeout once the sensors clear.

The **[RoadReady Pro](./Pro Crossing/manual.md)** keeps all the features of the Basic model, and adds the ability to control two or four quandrant gates using servos or motors (such as Circuitron Tortoises), and have [approach sensors](../Tips%20and%20Tricks/Articles/xing-basics.md#adding-approach-circuits), like the prototype, allowing the crossing to trigger long before the train arrives.

## Features

Both models of our RoadReady crossing controllers offer the following features:

* Plug-and-play detectors and speaker connections - no fiddly terminal blocks!
* Rock solid TrainSpotter optical detection - works in any ambient lighting conditions
* Universal Power - works from 8V to 24V DC or DCC
* Seven different built-in prototypical crossing bell sounds
* Included speaker with custom-designed resonance chamber
* Configurable island sensor timeouts
* Works with common anode (common positive) LED grade crossing signals
* Robust, protected signal output drivers will handle 1 amp of current
* Compatible with the [Track Expansion Module](Track Expansion Module/manual.md)

The [RoadReady Pro](./Pro Crossing/manual.md) adds the following features:

* Crossing gate control using servos or motors for two-quadrant or four-quadrant gates
* [Approach circuit logic](../Tips%20and%20Tricks/Articles/xing-basics.md#adding-approach-circuits) for up to two tracks
* Configurable approach sensor timeouts
* Lamp or LED emulation for the crossing signals
* A constant output in addition to left/right flashers, active when the lights are on
* Expandable with four more TrainSpotters for a second track, or with a [Track Expansion Module](Track Expansion Module/manual.md) for even more tracks.
